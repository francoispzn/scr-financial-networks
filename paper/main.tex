\chapter*{Abstract}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Abstract}
% ══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

This project adapts spectral coarse-graining (SCG), a graph renormalization procedure originally developed by Schmidt, Caccioli and Aste~\cite{schmidt2024spectral} for general complex networks, to the analysis of systemic risk in financial systems. We construct a 10-bank European interbank network from daily stock return correlations over the period 2021--2024 and develop an integrated framework combining three methodological components: (i) spectral coarse-graining with the Erd\H{o}s-R\'enyi null model gap test for statistically grounded dimensionality reduction; (ii) a temporal Graph Attention Network (GAT-LSTM)~\cite{velickovic2018graph} that predicts the evolution of spectral risk indicators from sequences of daily graph snapshots; and (iii) a multi-channel agent-based model (ABM) that simulates contagion through credit losses, funding liquidity stress, and fire-sale asset correlation channels.

Our empirical evaluation, conducted across 1,096 daily graph snapshots and multiple experimental configurations, yields several findings---most of them cautionary. The central result is that walk-forward cross-validation produces a mean $R^2 = -0.42 \pm 0.57$ for the GAT-LSTM, dramatically lower than the $R^2 = 0.382$ obtained from a single train/test split. The model achieves $R^2 = +0.69$ in one temporal fold but negative $R^2$ in four others, revealing strong regime dependence: the model captures patterns in specific market regimes but fails to generalise across the structural breaks of the 2021--2024 period. Feature ablation confirms that volatility is the dominant input ($\Delta R^2 = -1.08$ when removed), suggesting the GNN primarily learns volatility patterns rather than graph structure. Random matrix theory analysis reveals that 9 of 10 eigenvalues in the bank correlation matrix are consistent with noise, questioning whether spectral analysis of small financial networks is meaningful.

The SCG procedure achieves perfect diffusion reconstruction ($R^2 = 1.0$) across five network topologies, and the three-channel ABM demonstrates $10\times$ default amplification under severe liquidity stress. However, backtesting reveals that the proposed SCG risk indicator ($1 - \lambda_2/\rho$) anti-correlates with market volatility ($r = -0.63$), capturing network fragmentation rather than systemic stress. Bootstrap confidence intervals show that only algebraic connectivity ($\lambda_2$, CI: $[0.160, 0.275]$) and spectral gap (CI: $[0.122, 0.274]$) are significantly predictable; the spectral radius is not (CI includes zero). Conditional spectral indicators---volatility-regressed $\lambda_2$ and change-point detection---are explored as alternatives, and 3 structural breaks in the spectral time series are identified. These findings highlight that translating spectral diagnostics into operational risk indicators requires careful empirical validation, robust cross-validation, and networks substantially larger than 10 nodes. The framework is implemented as a modular Python package with an interactive Dash dashboard.
